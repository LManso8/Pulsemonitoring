# Interpretation Engine

## Overview
The Interpretation module acts as the normative decision engine of the GeoNode software. It isolates the raw mathematical outputs generated by the Digital Signal Processing pipeline and evaluates them against established civil engineering thresholds and seismological scales. 

To ensure high cohesion and maintainability, the interpretation logic is strictly decoupled into two specialized sub-modules: **Environmental** and **Seismic**.

---

## Directory Structure & Operating Modes

### 1. `/environmental` (Structural & Occupational Assessment)
This module evaluates high-frequency, localized vibrations to prevent structural damage and ensure human comfort.
* **Algorithm Logic:** It correlates the Peak Particle Velocity (PPV) or Peak Vector Sum (PVS) with the signal's Dominant Frequency (Hz) to calculate a dynamic, frequency-dependent safety limit.
* **Normative Frameworks Applied:**
  * **NP 2074 (Portuguese Standard):** Tri-orthogonal evaluation for Sensitive, Standard, and Reinforced structures.
  * **DIN 4150-3 (German Standard):** Single-axis peak evaluation for Historical, Residential, and Industrial buildings.
  * **BS 6472-1 / ISO 2631-2:** Continuous harmonic evaluation for human comfort regarding rotating machinery.
  * **Occupational Thresholds:** Static limits for Impulsive Detonations (0.30 mm/s) and Continuous Background Noise (0.15 mm/s).
* **Output:** A boolean compliance state (Alert/Safe) and the exact dynamic limit (mm/s) calculated for the specific frequency.

### 2. `/seismic` (Macroscopic Hazard Classification)
This module triggers during low-frequency, high-amplitude transient events, shifting the analytical focus from structural compliance to regional seismology.
* **Algorithm Logic:** It isolates the Peak Ground Velocity (PGV) mapped on the vertical axis (Z-axis) as the primary indicator of energy dissipation.
* **Normative Frameworks Applied:** * **Modified Mercalli Intensity (MMI):** Applies the empirical logarithmic conversion derived by *Wald et al. (1999)* to translate kinematic velocity into perceived human and structural impact.
* **Output:** The Roman numeral Intensity grade (I to IX+) accompanied by the scientific hazard description (e.g., "Instrumental", "Ligeiro", "Destrutivo").
